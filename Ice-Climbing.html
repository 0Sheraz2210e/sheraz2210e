What is Ice-Climbing?

Ice climbing is the activity of ascending inclined ice formations. Usually, ice climbing refers to roped and protected climbing of features such as icefalls, frozen waterfalls, and cliffs and rock slabs covered with ice refrozen from flows of water.





How risky Ice-Climbing Is?
In summary, ice-climbing is a bit less extreme than one may think. While there is a chance for serious trauma to occur, most injuries are mild. Because many injuries involve the head and face, wearing a helmet (required in our ice park) and face shield/goggles can prevent a significant number of injuries
